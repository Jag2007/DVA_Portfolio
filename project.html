DVA Portfolio Case Study

Impact of COVID-19 on Maternal & Newborn Health

A dashboard-based healthcare analytics project studying maternal mental health, pandemic stress, and neonatal outcomes during COVID-19.

GitHub Repository View Report Back To Projects

01

Problem statement and stakeholder context

Pregnancy during the COVID-19 pandemic created new health and psychological risks for mothers and newborns. The project asks how pandemic stress, anxiety, age, income, and education relate to birth outcomes such as preterm delivery, birth weight, delivery mode, and NICU admission.

The main stakeholders are maternal healthcare teams, public health planners, and prenatal program designers who need a clear view of which groups may require more mental health screening and targeted prenatal support.

02

Dataset source and scope

The analysis uses survey-based data from the Pregnancy during the COVID-19 Pandemic study conducted in Canada. The raw dataset contained about 10,773 records and 15 columns.

05

3-5 key insights

  • Anxiety and pandemic stress were elevated across the study population.
  • The cleaned dataset shows a 19.4% preterm birth rate, which is a key neonatal risk signal.
  • Young mothers under 25 and high-risk mothers over 35 showed relatively higher preterm proportions.
  • Lower-income groups showed a higher share of severe depression cases.
  • C-section deliveries showed higher NICU admission rates compared with vaginal deliveries.

06

Dashboard screenshot and links

The dashboard was built in Google Sheets using pivot tables, calculated KPIs, slicers, and dynamic charts. It supports filtering by NICU stay, preterm status, age category, income group, delivery mode, and education level.

07

Recommendations and expected impact

Screen Earlier

Add routine EPDS and anxiety screening during prenatal visits. Expected impact: earlier identification of mothers needing mental health support.

Target Support

Prioritize lower-income groups for counseling and support resources. Expected impact: better support for groups showing higher severe depression prevalence.

Flag Risk By Age

Use age-based prenatal risk flagging for younger and older mothers. Expected impact: closer monitoring of groups with higher preterm proportions.

Review Delivery Patterns

Review C-section decision patterns and NICU outcomes together. Expected impact: improved understanding of delivery-mode risk and neonatal care demand.
