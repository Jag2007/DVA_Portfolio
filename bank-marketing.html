Bank Marketing Campaign Analysis

A Tableau-based finance analytics project studying which customers are most likely to subscribe to a term deposit and how campaign strategy can improve conversion.

GitHub Repository Back To Projects

01

Problem statement and stakeholder context

Banks run direct marketing campaigns to sell term deposits, but broad outreach can waste time and reduce customer experience. This project asks which customer segments, contact channels, interaction histories, and economic conditions are most connected with campaign conversion.

The stakeholders are campaign managers, sales teams, and banking strategy teams who need to prioritize high-potential customers, choose better contact methods, and time campaigns more effectively.

02

Dataset source and scope

The project uses the UCI Bank Marketing Campaign dataset from a Portuguese banking institution. Each row represents one customer contact in a direct marketing campaign, with the target variable showing whether the customer subscribed to a term deposit.

03

Cleaning and transformation summary

  • Standardized raw column names into readable analysis columns.
  • Removed 12 exact duplicate rows.
  • Converted the subscription target from yes/no into a binary subscription flag.
  • Retained Unknown categories instead of imputing them, to avoid adding bias.
  • Capped extreme campaign contact counts at 10 to reduce outlier distortion.

Customer Features

Age group, education ordinal, job type, marital status, and loan flags.

Campaign Features

Call intensity, contact type, contact month, and previous contact count.

Context Features

Contact recency, prior success flag, and interest-rate environment.

05

3-5 key insights

  • Customers with prior campaign success convert at 65.11%, making previous success the strongest signal.
  • Cellular outreach converts at 14.74%, much higher than telephone outreach at 5.23%.
  • Conversion falls as call intensity rises, showing that excessive repeated contact is not effective.
  • Recently and moderately contacted customers convert much better than never-contacted customers.
  • Low-rate environments show stronger subscription behavior than high-rate environments.

07

Recommendations and expected impact

Prioritize Prior Success

Build campaign lists around customers with previous successful outcomes. Expected impact: higher conversion by focusing on the strongest repeat-subscription segment.

Use Cellular Outreach

Prefer cellular contact over telephone when available. Expected impact: stronger response rates through the better-performing contact channel.

Reduce Over-Contacting

Limit repeated calls after early attempts. Expected impact: reduced campaign fatigue and better use of sales team time.

Time Campaigns By Rates

Increase campaign intensity in low-rate environments. Expected impact: better timing when customers appear more open to term deposit subscriptions.
